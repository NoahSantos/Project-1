History of the W211 E63 AMG

After the production of the E 55 AMG ended in 2006, Mercedes introduced a new AMG model called the E63. Production for this car was from 2007-2009. This was AMG's first engine that they fully developed themselves. A 6.2 litre DOHC(dual overhead cam) v8 engine nicknamed the M156. The M156 was complemented with a new 7 speed automatic transmission that shifted faster than the snap of your fingers. Not using a supercharger or turbo, this car made 507 horsepower and 450 lb-ft of torque with a naturally aspirated engine. Although the E 63 has more horsepower than the E 55, it was slower than the E 55 in the ¼ mile and from 0-60. The E 63 did 0-60 in 4.5 seconds and the ¼ mile in 12.6 seconds. This engine would later on also have fatal engine issues. The head bolts of this car were made from a weak material causing them to erode over time and snap which would lead to head gasket failure which could blow the engine. It also had premature camshaft wear and camshaft adjuster problems. All of these gave the M156 engine a bad reputation and did not sell nearly as well as the E 55 AMG did. These engines were also not very tunable unless you added a form of forced induction like a turbo or supercharger which was not cheap. A supercharger for the M156 was around $12,000, not to mention the hassle of installing it with all the conversions needed for it to fit. It's safe to say that if you did not take care of your E 63, it will cut deep into your pockets to fix. Down below is a chart of the basic specs and prices of the car.
